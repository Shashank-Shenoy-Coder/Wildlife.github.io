HORNBILL

HORNBILL

Hornbill, any of approximately 60 species of Old World tropical birds constituting the family Bucerotidae.
They are noted for the presence, in a few species, of a bony casque, or helmet, surmounting the prominent bill.
They are typically large-headed, with thin necks, broad wings, and long tails. The plumage is brown or black,
usually with bold white markings.Hornbills range in size from 40 cm, in the smaller Tockus species,
to 160 cm, in the great hornbill. Several species, including the striking Rhinoceros hornbill, possess a
brightly coloured beak and casque.



Hornbills nest in cavities, usually in large trees. In all species except the two ground
hornbills, the male walls in the female on the nest, closing the hole with mud except for a small opening
through which he passes food. After the eggs hatch, the female breaks out, but the young
may be walled in again.

MASS

3-6 kg

SPEED

80-100 kmph

LIFESPAN

35-40 years

DIET

Fruits, Small Mammals
